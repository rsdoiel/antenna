The Antenna

An experiment in personal news aggregation

The Antenna is organized around lists of RSS, Atom and JSONfeeds that websites publish to promote their content. Right now Antenna is build the following lists

  • SoCal North, feeds from the Northern end of Southern California
    • socal_north.txt
  • Pacific, feeds from the Micronesia, Hawaii and the Pacific
    • pacific.txt
  • Mid Central, feeds from Central California’s in the vicinity of Merced County
  • Games, feeds about interactive games, games.txt
  • Retro Computing, feeds cover historic systems and retro computing
  • Columns, posts that frequently end up in my Pocket reading list, columns.txt
  • Tech Likely, miscellanious feeds, mostly computing related, tech_likely.txt
  • Motor Cycles, A test feed of interesting Motorcycle related news, motorcycles.txt
  • Weather, national weather information from NOAA’s weather.gov website.
    • weather.txt
  • Snapshots aggregation in waterhose built from snapshots/news.txt. It is very experimental and is focused on exploring Pagefind as a Personal Search Engine.

All the feed items are responsibility of the organizations that publish the feed. My choice is the specific feeds I select for my aggregation.

To read more about this experiment in the README file.

Antenna is a personal aggregation of items found around the web. Curated with skimmer and sqlite then rendered with Pandoc with search provided by PageFind.
