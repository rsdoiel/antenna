Ancient Roots of Cabbage RollsThe concept of stuffing cabbage leaves with meat and grains dates back centuries, with early versions appearing in the cuisines of the Ottoman Empire, Central Europe, and the Middle East. Instead of rice or barley, Irish cabbage rolls often incorporate potatoes, which have been a cornerstone of Irish cuisine since the 16th century.
