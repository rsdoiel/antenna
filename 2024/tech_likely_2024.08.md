<p>It was beyond its breaking point for a few years, actually... ever since I my average daily email volume increase from maybe 5-10 'important' emails to deal with to 50+.</p>

<p>My email-based TODO system used to go like this:</p>

<ol>
<li>Email myself things I deemed important enough to do the next day</li>
<li>Next morning, when I checked my email, knock off the top item in that list, and try to work down the list a bit</li>
<li>Anything else, forward that email again for the next day</li>
</ol>

<p>Once an item got maybe 5-10 <code>Fwd:</code>s in the Subject line, I would decide whether to nix the TODO item entirely, or move it off into a Trello boardâ€”in either case, likely to be forgotten forever.</p>

<p>I didn't say the system was <em>good</em>.</p>

<p>But it did work, before my inbox became full of <em>actually important stuff</em> relating to running my business.</p></div>
